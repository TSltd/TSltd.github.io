A servo-based CNC breakout board for the ESP32 DEVKITC (NodeMCU ESP32)

SERVOGRBL is a breakout board for the ESP32 DEVKITC to allow GRBL control of a 3 axis CNC machine using DC motors as servos instead of stepper motors. Currently it is in the first prototype itteration, version 0.1.

SERVOGRBL uses 3 Arduino Pro Minis in place of stepper drivers to convert STEP/DIR signals into PWM with encoder feedback on each channel.

This board builds on the incredible work of Bart Dring (GRBL_ESP32) and Miguel Sanchez (DCSERVO), to whom credit for its function is due.

Why use Servos instead of Stepper Motors?

There are various reasons why servos might be an interesting choice over steppers:

  • Cheaper

    • High torque brushed DC motors can be cheap, especially when recycled/reclaimed from machinery and automotive parts

  • Better torque
  • No lost steps- closed loop postional feedback

    • While stepper controllers deliver a list of commands and blindly expect the motors to keep up, a closed loop servo system listens to positional information from its sensors and always knows where its cutter head is in space. Servo systems do not suffer from errors due to 'lost' steps.
